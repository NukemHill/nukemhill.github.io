## About

I am a software architect and systems thinker with more than thirty years of experience across enterprise software, databases, distributed applications, mobile platforms, consulting, product design, and technical leadership.

My work begins with a question: **What is the shape of the system?**

Complex domains often appear chaotic because their important relationships are hidden inside fragmented data, institutional boundaries, historical rules, and representations that no longer fit the problem. I specialize in discovering the abstractions that make those systems understandable, then turning those abstractions into practical architectures and working software.

My current work includes:

- **ProjectOS and Signal Alliance:** a deterministic control-plane model for coordinated AI specialists, including durable identity, lifecycle protocols, authority boundaries, IPC, evidence, receipts, workspace binding, and the POSL orchestration language.
- **CivicSignal:** an AI-native civic-intelligence platform that transforms legislative and government data into understandable, provenance-preserving structures, beginning with Washington State.
- **Architecture Notebook:** public essays and case studies on complex systems, graph thinking, compiler architecture, agent coordination, civic intelligence, and durable engineering evidence.

I am particularly interested in software and AI systems architecture, principal/staff engineering, founding-engineer opportunities, consulting, civic technology, compilers and domain-specific languages, control planes, data architecture, and knowledge systems.

**Every complex system has a shape. Good software reveals it.**
