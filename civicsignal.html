CivicSignal

A civic-intelligence platform designed to make government understandable without flattening the procedures, relationships, and provenance that give it meaning.

The problem

Government is not incomprehensible because information is absent. It is incomprehensible because the information is fragmented across institutions, procedures, documents, calendars, votes, jurisdictions, and specialized language. A bill summary alone cannot explain what is happening, where it is happening, who is involved, or why it matters.

The shape

CivicSignal treats government as a connected system: representatives, districts, bills, versions, sponsors, committees, referrals, actions, votes, topics, constitutional provisions, and source evidence. The product is not simply a tracker. It is an attempt to compile public records into a civic model that people can navigate.

Government is noisy. Civic intelligence begins when the relationships become visible.

Implemented foundation

  • Washington legislative ingestion from state SOAP/XML services.
  • Normalized entities for states, districts, legislators, bills, bill versions, committees, memberships, and referrals.
  • Extensible state-oriented ingestion and normalization boundaries.
  • Procedural status translation that distinguishes lifecycle stage from institutional location.
  • Responsive workspaces for legislation, representatives, districts, and committees.
  • Pluggable state-neutral audit compiler with deterministic planning, findings, metrics, persistence, and previous-run comparison.
  • Operational Knowledge Objects with stable identity and lifecycle for audit findings.

The compiler model

CivicSignal is converging on a compiler-like execution graph: external sources are parsed, normalized, bound into canonical domain state, semantically audited, converted into Knowledge Objects, enriched through intelligence passes, and emitted through notifications, interfaces, and agents.

This model allows state-specific complexity to remain behind plugins while the system preserves a stable internal architecture. It also creates a path from raw public data to durable organizational knowledge without inventing a new ontology for every subsystem.

Long-term direction

The initial product focuses on Washington State, but the architecture is intended to support nationwide expansion. Future layers include personalized issue intelligence, significance scoring, constitutional intelligence, operational drift detection, and graph-native views of how legislation, people, institutions, money, geography, and policy connect.

Design principle: simplify the citizen’s experience, not the underlying truth. The system should preserve enough procedural and evidentiary structure to explain how it reached every conclusion.
