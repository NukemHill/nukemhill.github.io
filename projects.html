Selected Work

Projects

A set of long-running explorations in AI systems, civic technology, compilers, coordination, and knowledge architecture.

ProjectOS

A deterministic operating environment for coordinating AI specialists through explicit lifecycle commands, durable state, evidence gates, and execution receipts. Current work includes POSL, a human-readable orchestration language compiled into resolved execution packets.

Active · Implemented

CivicSignal

An AI-native civic intelligence platform designed to help citizens understand legislation, government activity, representation, and policy impact. Initial implementation focuses on Washington State legislative data and extensible state-by-state architecture.

Active · Implemented

Signal Alliance

A multi-agent engineering and project-management environment for coordinating specialized AI Nodes. It provides the durable control-plane context around ProjectOS, including roles, relays, work ownership, dependencies, and portfolio state.

Active · Implemented

JournalNexus

An early-stage concept exploring how scholarly publishers and AI systems might participate in a provenance-preserving discovery network. The work is currently limited to problem framing and high-level architectural exploration.

What connects them

These projects operate in different domains, but they share a common method: identify the structures hidden inside a difficult system, preserve provenance, create a durable intermediate representation, and expose useful views without pretending that a projection is the whole truth.
