A common method

  1. Begin with the system, not the proposed interface.
  2. Identify the relationships and invariants that give it form.
  3. Choose an abstraction that preserves meaning rather than merely discarding detail.
  4. Build a durable representation that humans and machines can inspect.
  5. Expose projections suited to a task without confusing the projection with the underlying truth.
The implementation is not the starting point. First find the shape. Then choose the machinery that can preserve and reveal it.
